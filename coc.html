Manzil Parwaaz Code of Conduct

Note that this code of conduct applies for every core-team and volunteer member of Manzil Parwaaz. It will serve as an aid to assist members in acclimating to formal norms. Violating the code of conduct will result in varying degrees of consequences, depending on the violated clause

Volunteer Procedural Analysis

Volunteers will be expected to uphold a consistent work ethic and demonstrate cooperation and consideration towards their fellow members. In order to foster a positive and highly productive atmosphere, we encourage any level of social interaction or manifestation of extroverted behavior. The ensuing points should be borne in mind during one's tenure at Manzil Parwaaz:

  • Volunteers are expected to actively and sincerely participate with unwavering commitment and dedication.
  • It is imperative to treat underprivileged and/or marginalized communities as equals, avoiding any condescending behavior or language. Moreover, all forms of disputes, whether physical or verbal, must be avoided at all times.
  • Volunteers must refrain from engaging in any behavior or speech that is demeaning, discriminatory, or harassing. Additionally, they are responsible for being attentive to their surroundings and fellow participants, promptly informing project heads or leadership members of any hazardous situations or violations of this Code of Conduct, no matter how seemingly inconsequential.
  • When a dress code is in place, volunteers are required to stick to it respectfully. This consideration is necessary to demonstrate respect towards the backgrounds and beliefs of underprivileged communities during interactions with them.
  • To attain an official Manzil Parwaaz affiliation certificate, a volunteer must aid in conducting at least two drives, and should participate in at least four.

Intolerable Actions

Failure to abide by the aforementioned rules and regulations, combined with engaging in the following acts will result in removal or blacklisting from Manzil Parwaaz:

Acts resulting in removal

  • Engaging in inappropriate and unnecessary conversations. This includes display of racism, sexism and other violating actions through speech.
  • Being late, or failing to show up to an activity without mentioning it prior (and having the excuse approved).

Acts resulting in being blacklisted:

  • Engaging in any degree of physical violence or malice.
  • Recording or photographing someone without their consent.
  • Partaking in any form of PDA(public display of affection)
  • Smoking, vaping or possessing any drug-related substance.
  • Blackmailing or 'gaslighting' any other member.
  • Inappropriately coming into contact with another individual intentionally,
  • Stalking or intimidating anyone in person, or online.
  • The president of Manzil Parwaaz reserves the right to blacklist anyone or refuse anyone a completion certificate if he sees fit.
